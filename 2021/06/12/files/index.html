Active Questions

Why is 'natural' almost always equated to 'good'?

Need to explore philosophically, historically, culturally, etc.
  • Is this association cyclical i.e. is the association of 'naturalness' with 'goodness' just a current thing?
  • In what ways can biology be considered a religion? Here, I mean a religion with a proper deity, where the deity is used to explain a black box system.
  • (+1 for nature as good) Hipprocratic medical practitioners believed in 'letting Nature do the work' - they focused on removing impediments that block Nature from healing the body.
  • (+0 for nature as good) Aristotle believed that Nature creates nothing in excess - any removed component means the system fails to work optimally. Similar idea of Nature being an all-knowing architect?
  • Descartes' proofs of God are cyclical, but they indicate the de facto premise that God is good. Briefly, Descartes' proofs of God depend on his being able to see God's existence clearly and distinctly. A malevolent God would try to deceive him, but since he sees clearly and distinctly, God is good.
  • All these weird but fascinating movements, trying to get back to pre-capitalist lifestyles (diets, barefoot running movement, etc.). There's no question that we've become much more sedentary, but the immediate reflex to 'tend towards the natural way' is cool.

How do you resolve contradictions?

Strictly speaking, contradictions cannot exist in the real world (or any world, actually).
  • Contradictions are basically things that can't exist in the same frame of reference.
  • Change frames of reference. Everything is relative, so by changing the reference point, you are able to resolve contradictions.
